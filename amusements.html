Printed newspapers are becoming an anachronism in the age of digital newsfeed, and most newspapers have shifted their focus on to the web. My degree project, The Daily Exchange, is the namesake of an 1800s Baltimore newspaper, and a semester-long process of mimicking that shift.

The Daily Exchange was chosen as the source of content due to its relatively impartial reporting and thus reflection of life in Baltimore during the mid-1800s. It ran from 1858 to 1861, and it began as a publication that was highly critical of The Know-Nothing Party who strongly opposed the presence of immigrants, particularly in political offices and competition for jobs.

Used Up, Milly, and The Count of Monte Cristo at The Holliday Street Theatre!

Admission
$1 The Gallery will be open on Friday evening at 25 cents. All the rest of the house $1. The doors will open at 6½ o’clock, the curtain rising at 7½ precisely.

The testimonial benefit to Mr. John T. Ford, will take place on Friday evening, December 31, 1858, and be continued on the afternoon of January 1st, 1859. The exquisite actress, Miss Agnes Robertson, the celebrated dramatist and occasional actor, Mr. Dion Boucicault, the eminent musical celebrity, Mr. Theodore Ahrend, and the entire Dramatic Company of the Theatre will appear.

The modern comedy of Used Up in which Mr. Boucicault will make his first and only appearance for the present at this Theatre. The delightful pastoral comedy, entitled Milly, the Maid with the Milking Pail in which Ms. Agnes Robertson will make her first and only appearance for the present at this Theatre.

From the Great Masters on the Violoncello by Mr. Theodore Ahrend, (by the kind consent of Mr. Leutbecher.) To conclude with the carnival scene of Monte Cristo in which the Carlo Family, who stand unequalled in the world, and the wonder of the age. Herr Spingalgen will appear, in addition to the Dramatic Company and auxiliaries.

Order of Performances: Friday — Used Up, Musical Selections. Milly, the Carnival. Saturday, at 2 o’clock — The Carnival and other performances. On Saturday evening, the play of Monte Cristo and the Carnival at the old prices — 50 and 25 cents. In preparation — “The Phantom.”

The Wife! A Quiet Family! at The Holliday Street Theatre.

Admission
Dress circle and parquette: 50 cents; Family circle: 25 cents; Colored gallery: 25 cents. No extra change for reserved seats. Doors open at 6 ¾ — to commence at 7 ¾ o’clock.

Lessees: Kunkel & Co., Manager: John T. Ford. Engagement of the popular American actress, Mrs. Julia Dean Hayne, on this Tuesday evening will be performed The Wife! Starring Mrs. Julia Dean Hayne as Mariana, Mr. H.F. Daly as Julian St. Pierre, and Miss Partington as Olio Dance. To conclude with A Quiet Family, starring Mr. Fiske as Bibbs and Miss Jefferson as Snarley.

Mrs. Julia Dean Hayne made her New York debut at the Bowery Theatre in the principal role of Julia in the comedy The Hunchback, a part that she almost monopolized on the American stage. Youthful, graceful, delicately pretty, with a slight Hibernian cast of features, she at once awakened the public interest, and her intelligence and graceful study lent a charm to her performance which soon carried her to a point of popularity rarely exceeded.

Visual Arts

India and the Late Mutiny illustration series at The Maryland Institute!

Admission
Doors open at 6 ½ the paintings will move at 7 ½ o’clock. Admission 25 cents. Children until ten years 10 cents.

Great success! Crowded nightly! Wednesday evening, Dec. 29th, and every evening during the week at 7 ½ o’clock, and on Wednesday and Saturday afternoons at 3 o’clock, the large mammoth moving illustrations of India and the Late Mutiny, showing some of the most interesting scenes in the late War with India — Tremendous Charges of Artillery — Burning of Aahabad — Mothers Destroying their Children — The Mutineers Murdering the English Troops, Women and Children — Mrs. Wheeler Defending Herself Against the Rebels — Jessie Brown, et cetera.
